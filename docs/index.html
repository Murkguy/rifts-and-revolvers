Chapter One
The Shattered Frontier
Setting · Lore · The World That Broke
"The Rifts opened in '72. The railroad barons said it was God's gift. The machines that crawled out said otherwise. The Seers just smiled." — Overheard at the Dustfall Saloon, Dimension Seven

Rifts & Revolvers is set in the Shattered Frontier — a patchwork of overlapping dimensions where the American West collides with futures, pasts, and places that have no right to exist.

Dimensional Rifts tear through reality without warning, depositing clockwork automatons, spell-slingers, and horrors into dusty frontier towns that already had plenty of their own problems. Players take on the role of Frontier folk — gunslingers, healers, tricksters, brawlers, and wanderers — each navigating a world where two things are always true: the dice tell your story, and the cards change it.

How the Game Works — Three Layers

Every action runs through three interlocking systems. They don't overlap — each one does a specific job.

🎲 Dice — The Story

Poker dice resolve what happens. Roll five dice, build the best poker hand across up to three rolls, and the result tells you whether you succeeded and by how much. Only roll when failure has a real consequence. If you can't describe what losing looks like before you roll, don't roll.

🃏 Cards — The Edge

A double deck of playing cards (104 cards) sits at the center of the table. Every player holds a secret five-card Persistent Hand dealt at session start. Cards don't tell the story — they bend it. Discard a card to fuel a reroll, trigger a class ability, or feed the GM's pressure engine.

🎰 Chips — Your Status

Five colors of poker chips track your health, nerve, luck, wealth, and the dimensional debt accumulating against your soul. They sit in front of you and tell the table exactly what kind of shape you're in — without anyone rolling a thing.

🎩 The GM Is the House

The GM never rolls dice against players. The GM sets stakes using Fate Chips placed in the center of the table. Players decide whether to match them and roll, or walk away. The GM earns Fate Chips every time a player recharges their card hand — the more players push, the more pressure the GM can apply.

Chapter Three
The Stack — Stress
One track · three states · rest to recover
"I've been shot, swindled, and dragged through a Rift backwards. I'm not fine. I'm still here. Those aren't the same thing."

Every hit lands in the same place. The Frontier doesn't file bullets, humiliations, and horrors separately. Stress is what you have left. Your class determines how much you start with. When it runs out, you go down.

The Three States
StateThresholdEffect
ClearStress taken below half maximumNo effect
VulnerableStress taken exceeds half maximum (round down)All incoming Stress +1 from any source
BrokenStress at maximumCombat: Taken Out — opponent narrates fate. Elsewhere: cannot initiate action next scene.

Rest scenes restore 3 Stress. Stress cannot exceed class maximum. Stress does not refresh at session start — earn it back.

The Price · Void Marks · What You Owe
You gain these — they are a liability. The GM can add Black chips to any stakes as a dimensional cost; to attempt the action, you accept those chips into your stack. Some class abilities generate them as their price. Never recovered through rest.

The GM's Fate Pool

The GM holds a pool of Fate Chips — a neutral marker. Starts each session at zero. The GM gains 1 Fate Chip per card drawn by any player through any mechanic. Every card you pull to improve your hand feeds the pressure engine.

How the GM Earns & Spends

Earns: 1 Fate Chip per card drawn by any player. This is the core loop — players need better cards, but getting them feeds the GM.

Spends: Redraw own 5-card hand (3 chips). Activate certain GM face card abilities.

Sets stakes: Place Fate Chips in the center. Players commit Stress to attempt the action. Success returns it. Failure doesn't.

Chapter Four
The Poker Dice System
5 dice · 3 rolls · best hand wins · only roll when failure has a consequence

All resolution uses five poker dice — each face showing 9, 10, J, Q, K, A. Lock dice between rolls to build the best poker hand you can in up to three throws.

The Roll Sequence
1
Declare Stakes. Name what you're risking. The GM sets Fate Chips. Match them or walk away.
2
Roll All Five. Throw your full pool.
3
Lock & Reroll. Set aside keepers face-up. Roll the rest. Repeat once more — 3 rolls total.
4
Reveal. Compare your final hand to the required threshold, or to your opponent's hand.
→
Narrate. Winner narrates first. Loser responds. Ties: both narrate simultaneously.

Hand Rankings

With only six die faces, only two Straights exist: 9-10-J-Q-K and 10-J-Q-K-A. Straights are the gambler's hand — near-impossible with optimal locking, rewarded with a Blue chip.

Dice Modifiers

All four mechanics are universal — any class can use them. What differs is the trigger.

LOCK — Pre-Roll Certainty

Declare one or more dice values before rolling. Place them face-up. You chose your outcome before chance had a word. Pre-locks cannot be unlocked. Suits: Gunslinger, Cogwright.

WILD DIE — Post-Roll Fix

After your final roll, set one die to any value. This happens after you see results — you're fixing what went wrong, not preventing it. Suits: Brawler, Charlatan.

REROLL — Extra Chances

Discard a number card (2–10) of matching suit from your hand to gain one extra reroll. Any matching-suit number card = one reroll, regardless of value. Stack multiple discards for multiple rerolls.

WILD CARD — Rift Exclusive

One die value becomes equal to Ace for the rest of the scene. Affects everyone — players and NPCs. Exclusive to the Seer (costs a Diamond face card) and the GM (costs 2 Fate Chips). Chaos Track +1. Cannot be countered.

BUST — The Gambler's Risk

After your second roll: discard all locked dice, roll all five fresh. Win: gain 1 Blue chip (passes left if held). Lose: opponent acts first automatically, you take 2 Stress from the whiplash. The Frontier punishes gambling that doesn't pay.

Chapter Five
The Persistent Hand
5 secret cards · pass left · spend to act · never show, never tell
"Nobody said a word. The Cogwright slid one card left without looking up. The Charlatan palmed it, her expression unchanged. Somewhere across the table, the Gunslinger understood something had changed."

At session start, every player — and the GM — is dealt five cards from a shuffled double deck (104 cards). This is your Persistent Hand, held all session. Cards are spent, passed, and burned. Never shown. Never discussed.

Law 1 — Secrecy

Never show your cards. Never describe them verbally — not suit, value, or count. Meaningful glances are permitted. Sighing is permitted. Sliding a card toward someone is permitted. Truth about your hand is not. You may say "someone pass to the Doctor" — but not what you're passing or why.

Law 2 — The Pass

Once per scene, pass one card face-down to the player on your left only. They must accept it. They then discard one card face-down to return to five. They choose which to discard. No passing right, across, or back. Holding all session is valid — and lonely.

Law 3 — The Discard Economy
Card TypeIn Your SuitOut of Suit
Number (2–10)Trigger class ability OR 1 reroll on matching action1 reroll on matching-suit action only
Face (J/Q/K/A)Trigger named class ability — cannot use for rerollsPass left. Cannot discard cheaply.
Diamond face (any)Rift Reading OR Seer Wild CardRift Reading — costs +1 Black chip for non-Seers
JokerPlay any time: reshape hand / trigger Rift Tear / Walk Away. Never reshuffled.

Resonance — The Double Deck

With 104 cards, the same card exists twice. If two players discard the same card simultaneously: Resonance triggers — both class effects fire at full strength, both gain 1 Blue chip, Chaos Track +1. The duplicate shouldn't exist. The Frontier notices.

Opening hand odds: ~34% chance of holding a face card of your suit. ~10% chance of suit majority (3+ of your suit). The Pass exists because you rarely open with what you need.

Recharge

Your hand shrinks as you spend. Recharge is a class ability — not automatic. When you recharge, draw from the Frontier Deck until you hold five again. Every card drawn gives the GM 1 Fate Chip. This is the core pressure loop.

Chapter Six
Class Abilities
Number cards trigger core abilities · Face cards unlock named powers

Number cards (2–10) of your suit trigger scaled abilities. Face cards trigger named abilities that work differently — not just stronger, but accessing angles number cards cannot reach.

♥ Hearts Classes

Doctor — Core: Treat

Discard any Hearts number card targeting an ally: low (2–5) reduce Stress by 1, mid (6–9) reduce by 2, 10 reduces by 3. Treatment works regardless of source — wounds, broken nerve, nightmares. Only class that reduces Stress through card play outside Rest scenes.

Recharge: After treating an ally (reducing any Stress), draw 1 card. Teammate Recharge: Discard a Hearts face card to let one ally draw back to 5 cards.

CardNamed Ability
J♥Stabilize: Ally at zero White is not Downed this scene. Stays at 1 White until scene end.
Q♥Diagnosis: Name a condition — GM confirms or denies truthfully. Lock one die on your next roll.
K♥Full Recovery: One ally's Stress resets to zero immediately. Doctor gains 1 Black chip.
A♥Miracle: Reverse one consequence this scene — a wound, Stress taken, a failed roll's result. Chaos Track +2.
Charlatan — Core: Performance

Discard any Hearts number card during a social action to force one target to reroll their highest die.

Wild Die: While in a false identity or cover, discard any Hearts card to access a Wild Die — no extra cost. Recharge: Draw 1 card after successfully conning resources or information.

CardNamed Ability
J♥The Sob Story: Target cannot spend Stress for any ability this scene.
Q♥Snake Oil: Target reduces Stress by 2 now, takes 3 Stress at start of next scene when it wears off.
K♥The Long Con: False identity lasts the session. Social rolls count as one rank higher. Breaks on combat hit.
A♥The Vanish: Exit any scene instantly — no consequence. Chips taken remain. Gain 1 Black chip.

♠ Spades Classes

Gunslinger — Core: Called Shot

Discard any Spades number card before rolling in a Duel or Shootout to Lock one die to any value before the roll begins. You declared the shot before you took it.

Recharge: Win a Duel with Full House or better — draw 1 card and reduce Stress by 1.

CardNamed Ability
J♠Trick Shot: Lock two dice before rolling instead of one.
Q♠Read the Room: Opponent reveals one die they intend to lock. You may change a lock in response.
K♠The Reputation: Name a consequence — if you win by two ranks or more, it happens without narration.
A♠The Legend: Roll 6 dice this Duel, drop the lowest. Once per session.
Bandit — Core: Disrupt

Discard any Spades number card during an opponent's roll sequence to force them to unlock one currently locked die. Their certainty becomes doubt.

Recharge: Successfully steal resources — draw 1 card. Reduce Stress by rank difference after any successful ambush or theft (minimum 1).

CardNamed Ability
J♠Ambush: You act before initiative is rolled this scene. Always first.
Q♠Inside Job: Name one item, chip type, or info a target carries. You take it. No roll. They notice at scene end.
K♠Smoke and Mirrors: All enemies reroll their highest die this round.
A♠The Setup: Plant a trap. At any point later this session, reveal it to make one roll automatically succeed.

♣ Clubs Classes

Cogwright — Core: Prepared

During any scene where the Cogwright has had time to prepare (not in immediate combat), discard any Clubs number card to Lock one die before rolling.

Recharge: After modifying, repairing, or building something — draw 1 card. Sell or deploy a working device to reduce Stress by 1–4 (GM determines complexity).

CardNamed Ability
J♣Overclock: One ally's device improves — they Lock one die on their next roll at no card cost.
Q♣Structural Analysis: Identify the exact value of any Cover Card before it's revealed.
K♣Invention: Create a one-use device replicating any basic class ability from any class.
A♣The Machine: Build something that changes the scene permanently. GM cannot remove it this session.
Brawler — Core: Wild Swing

In any physical confrontation, discard any Clubs card to access a Wild Die — set one die to any value after rolling. The Brawler doesn't aim. They adjust.

Recharge: Win any physical exchange — reduce Stress by 1 and draw 1 card. Winning hurts less than losing.

CardNamed Ability
J♣Haymaker: This hit cannot be negated by card play. It lands.
Q♣Take a Hit: Absorb an ally's damage. You take it plus 1 extra Stress.
K♣Last Stand: At your Stress maximum, make one final roll. Three of a Kind or better: reduce Stress by 3. Fail: fall.
A♣Unstoppable: Nothing stops your next physical action this round. No chips, cards, or abilities can prevent it.

♦ Diamonds Classes

Seer — Core: Read the Thread

Discard any Diamonds card to ask the GM one yes/no question about what's about to happen. Low (2–5): approximate answer. Mid (6–9): clear answer. 10: answer plus one detail.

Wild Card: Discard a Diamonds face card to declare one die value = Ace for the scene. Table-wide, cannot be countered.
Recharge: Publicly predict an outcome before a roll — if the dice confirm it, draw 1 card.

CardNamed Ability
J♦Glimpse: Look at the top 3 cards of the Frontier Deck. Return in any order.
Q♦Foresight: Name a hand rank — if anyone rolls it this scene, gain 2 Blue chips.
K♦Rewrite: After a roll is announced, shift the outcome one hand rank in either direction.
A♦The Vision: Stop the scene. Everyone shares a vision of one possible future. Chaos Track +2. Seer gains 1 Black.
Drifter — Core: Phase

Discard any Diamonds number card to move to any position in the scene — including occupied Cover positions. No roll required. Current occupant is displaced to open ground.

Recharge: Escape a confrontation without taking a hit — draw 1 card and reduce Stress by 1.

CardNamed Ability
J♦Not There: Retroactively declare you weren't in your stated position this round. One attack misses.
Q♦Someone Else's Problem: Transfer one active consequence from yourself to any NPC in the scene.
K♦Between Worlds: Exit the scene and reenter at any point later in the session at a location of your choosing.
A♦Rift Step: You and one ally reappear anywhere in the current or adjacent scene. Chaos Track +2. Gain 2 Black chips.
Chapter Seven
The Rift — Reading Dimensional Signs
Diamond cards · dimensional intelligence · always costs something
"The card came up Diamond. Not his class. Not his suit. But it was an Ace and the Rift was right there and he understood, for a moment, what the crackle in the air was saying."
Who Can Read the Rift

Seer: Any Diamond card (2–Ace). Full range available. This is their core identity.

All other classes: Only a face card of Diamonds (J♦, Q♦, K♦, A♦). Also costs 1 Black chip — the Rift charges for unsanctioned access.

Card UsedWhoResult

Chapter Nine
Scene Types — The Minigames
Everyone can enter every scene · Classes provide advantages, not gatekeeping
Scene One · Spades
High Noon — The Duel
"The street went quiet. The kind of quiet that isn't peaceful — the kind that's waiting."

Structured one-on-one contest. Any class can duel — the Gunslinger's cards just make them terrifying at it.

1
Draw Phase. Each duelist rolls one die. Higher result chooses to go first or second. Going first means locking without seeing your opponent's choices.
2
The Hand. Both roll their full 3-roll sequence simultaneously, locked dice hidden.
3
Showdown. Reveal simultaneously. Resolve by hand ranking.
4
Stress. Loser takes Stress equal to rank difference (min 1, max 4 per exchange). Going Vulnerable mid-Duel raises all subsequent costs by 1.
↺
Repeat or Resolve. If neither is Broken, restart from Draw Phase. Stress does not restore mid-Duel.
White Chips Lost (cumulative)ConditionEffect
1–2GrazedLose 1 card draw on next recharge
3–4WingedRoll only 4 dice until treated
5–6BleedingRoll only 3 dice; no card abilities this scene
7+DownedOut of Duel. Opponent decides your fate.
Quick Draw

Skip Draw Phase, go first automatically. Cost: discard your highest Spades card, or take 1 Stress if you have no Spades. Roll once, lock, reveal. Win: cinematic single shot. Lose by even one rank: take 3 Stress immediately. High risk. High drama.

Gambit Card

Before any Duel, each duelist draws one card as a secret Gambit. Play it "Before the draw" (bonus die result), "On the pull" (force opponent to unlock a die), or "At the showdown" (if it matches your suit, flip the outcome for 2 Black chips). Face card Gambits: Jacks distract, Queens read minds, Kings bluff, Aces add a sixth die.

Scene Two · All Classes
Lead & Lightning — The Shootout
"Twelve guns, two sides, one street. The Cogwright dove behind a steam cart. The Drifter was already gone."

Three or more combatants. Unlike the Duel, a Shootout is spatial and chaotic — positioning matters, the Chaos Track climbs, and the Frontier can tear open at any moment.

Initiative: All combatants roll one die. Highest goes first; ties act simultaneously. Each turn: Shot (roll against one target) or Cover (defensive benefits, cannot attack).

Cover Cards — The Position Game

At scene start the GM draws one card per two combatants (round up), placed face-down as Cover Positions. Cards reveal when first attacked or abandoned.

The Chaos Track

Starts at 0. Rises: Bust declared (+1), Wild Card called (+1), Five of a Kind rolled (+1), Seer/Drifter face card dimensional effect (+1 or +2). At Chaos 5, roll a die for the Rift Tear:

Scene Three · Clubs
Last Call — The Bar Brawl
"Nobody drew a gun. That's how you knew it was serious."

Close-quarters chaos. No Cover Cards. No initiative order. Everything happens simultaneously. The Brawler is home. Everyone else is surviving the furniture. Chaos Track starts at 2.

Simultaneous Card Exchange

All participants declare targets and play one card face-down simultaneously. Reveal together. Higher value wins the exchange.

MatchupResult

Lower valueTheir hit lands. You take 1 Stress.
Same value, different suitBoth hits land. Both take 1 Stress.
Same value, same suitResonance. No hits. Both gain 1 Blue chip (passes left if held).

Two face cards clashBoth effects trigger. Roll dice for priority if they conflict.
Brawler's Advantage

Any Clubs card (2–10) played by a Brawler in a Bar Brawl also grants a free Wild Die on their next roll — no extra discard. A 3 of Clubs in a Brawler's hand during a bar fight outperforms a King of Spades in anyone else's.

Escalation

After each round, roll one die: 9 — someone draws a gun, becomes a Shootout. A — someone goes through a window, scene ends outside. Otherwise, continue.

Scene Four · Hearts
Words Like Weapons — The Confrontation
"She didn't threaten him. She didn't need to. She just described, very quietly, what would happen next."

Social pressure, persuasion, seduction, intimidation, negotiation. The Charlatan and Seer dominate here — but a Gunslinger's reputation and a Brawler's presence are real factors too.

Stakes & Outcome

The GM sets Fate Chips as usual. The chip type signals the confrontation: Red = nerve cost, Green = material stakes, White = this confrontation has become dangerous. Hand ranking determines outcome quality — Full House wins decisively, One Pair barely gets there.

The Charlatan's Stage

While in a false identity or working con, the Charlatan's Hearts cards cost nothing to trigger — no discard. They are in their element. Cards flow naturally.

Romance — A Special Confrontation

Both parties roll. The hand ranking describes the quality of the moment, not a winner. Both reduce Stress by 1 on Three of a Kind or better — the moment restores something.

The GM's Hand

The GM holds five cards like every player. Not class-specific — abilities span all suits. Their face cards alter scene structure, not class abilities.

GM Face Card Effects
CardEffect
Any JackComplication: An unexpected element enters the scene — not hostile, just complicating.
Any QueenEscalation: Chaos Track +1. One permanent scene element changes.
Any KingThe Other Player: A named NPC enters with their own agenda. They have a scale position and their own motivations — not the GM's puppet.
Any AceThe World Speaks: Every character experiences a vision, memory, or dimensional bleed simultaneously. Pure story, no mechanical effect.
JokerThe Frontier Decides: Pass to the table. Players vote on the complication. GM cannot veto.
The Fate Cut

At the start of each scene, the GM secretly draws one card and holds it face-down. Reveal it when players feel too comfortable or the scene stalls. Unused Fate Cuts are discarded. The Frontier moves on.
